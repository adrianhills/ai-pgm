Agent workflows

I work. My agents work. At the same time.

I don't wait for dedicated time at my desk. While I'm in meetings or heads-down, my agents are already working — firing on events as they happen all day, and on a schedule overnight. I wake and surface from meetings to a short list of decisions, not a backlog.

Agents draft and flag. I own every decision, all Sev-1/2, and external comms. Nothing ships without me.

How it runs

Three triggers, one gate

The same skills I invoke by hand also run on their own — given a trigger and a human gate.

Event-triggered

A meeting ends, a Slack thread escalates, a PRD changes — the right skill fires in the moment, while I'm still in the room.

Scheduled

An overnight sweep on a cron — the watchdog scans every program and only speaks up when something crosses a threshold.

Supervised

Anything that posts or decides stops at me. The agent drafts and queues; I approve. Sev-1/2 and external comms are always mine.

Quiet is the default. Agents act only when something crosses a threshold — so most of what they do, I never have to see. Grounded in my real watchdog skill: a scheduled scan in Supervised Execute mode, logging every run to log.ndjson. Illustrative numbers; the structure — the triggers, the parallelism, the human gate — is real.
